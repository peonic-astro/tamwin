## Burlap
**Area:** Halfling farmlands region
**Type:** Small town
**Description:** Peaceful halfling community built into rolling hills. Neat gardens, cozy round doors built into hillsides, smoke rising from chimneys. Predominantly agrarian with fields of crops surrounding the town. The air smells of pipe-weed, fresh bread, and turned earth.
**Occupants:** ~200 halflings, mostly farmers and craftspeople
**Notable Features:**
- **Bagshott Row:** A row of comfortable hillside dwellings where the Badgerfoot family lives
- **Town Watch Station:** Where Tamwin worked before leaving
- Safe, law-abiding community with little crime
**First Visited:** Tamwin's hometown (lifelong residence until Day 1)

## Millstone
**Area:** ~3 miles from Burlap along the Old Road
**Type:** Village (larger than Burlap, ~300 people)
**Description:** Stone and timber buildings clustered around central square with well. Wooden palisade surrounds most of the village (not high, but enough for wolves). Mill with waterwheel at far edge where stream cuts through. Smoke from chimneys, muddy streets in rain.
**Occupants:** ~300 people (farmers, craftspeople, merchants)
**Notable Features:**
- **Central Square:** Well, gathering area
- **Mill:** Waterwheel on stream, village namesake
- **Wooden palisade:** Basic defensive wall
- **Gate with watchman:** Gareth on duty
- **Sack and Bushel inn:** Main/only inn (see below)
- Village headman (not yet met) looking for militia volunteers
**First Visited:** Day 1 evening (though Tamwin was here 2-3 seasons ago for summer fair)
**Notes:** Roads around Millstone have been dangerous lately - bandit activity

## The Sack and Bushel (Millstone Inn)
**Area:** Millstone village, near central square
**Type:** Inn and tavern
**Description:** Low-ceilinged common room with heavy oak beams, stone hearth at one end. Well-lit, warm, comfortable. Upstairs rooms are small but clean (narrow beds, wool blankets, washbasins). Sign outside shows painted grain sack.
**Occupants:** Joram (innkeeper), his wife (healer), various locals and travelers
**Notable Features:**
- Only inn/tavern in Millstone
- Good food (stew, bread, ale) and clean rooms
- Joram's wife is skilled healer
- Common room serves as gathering place for locals
**First Visited:** Day 1 evening
**Notes:** Tamwin has room here (paid by Wellan as thanks)

## Forest Clearing (Bandit Ambush Site)
**Area:** ~1 mile from Millstone on Old Road, off road in forest
**Type:** Small clearing in oak/ash forest
**Description:** Small clearing ~30 feet across. Site of bandit ambush where Wellan was held captive. One dead bandit (Bandit 2) left here after combat.
**Occupants:** Body of dead bandit (Arrow wounds), scattered evidence of camp
**Notable Features:**
- Site of Tamwin's first kill
- Dead bandit's body still here (Wellan mentioned reporting to village headman)
- Drag marks and tracks from bandits
**First Visited:** Day 1 midday
**Notes:** Two bandits (Bandits 1 & 3) fled into surrounding forest, still at large

## Old Road (Trade Road)
**Area:** Between Burlap and Millstone
**Type:** Well-traveled dirt road
**Description:** Winding road through farmland and forest patches. Wide enough for wagons, packed earth with grass growing along edges. Maintained near settlements but rougher in wilderness. Becomes muddy in rain.
**Occupants:** Occasional travelers, merchants, farmers - but dangerous lately
**Notable Features:**
- Connects Burlap to Millstone (3 miles) and beyond
- **Milestone marker** ~3 miles from Millstone (where old man was sleeping)
- **Wellan's cart** still on road with broken wheel, ~1 mile from Millstone
- Recent bandit activity makes travel dangerous
**First Visited:** Day 1 morning
**Notes:** Has gotten more dangerous in past year per Wellan
